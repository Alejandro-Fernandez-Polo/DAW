Route 66
Stage 1: Chicago (Illinois) - Springfield (Illinois)

After picking up our rental car, the first kilometers of the route are thrilling. Everything catches your attention; everything deserves a photo. Unfortunately, we had the bad luck of experiencing heavy rain that first day, but we still enjoyed it. First stop: Joliet. We spent the night in Springfield (Illinois) since, being the first stage, we preferred to fully enjoy everything, stopping at every curious corner we came across.

Stage 2: Springfield (Illinois) - Saint Louis (Missouri)

A short but beautiful stage, full of U.S. history and traditional Route 66 places. Saint Louis welcomed us under construction, but it didn't matter because we still enjoyed its iconic arch (even if we couldn't go to the top). Here, we tried one of the best rib dishes at Pappyâ€™s Smokehouse.

Stage 3: Saint Louis (Missouri) - Springfield (Missouri)

This stretch captivated us with its landscapes full of nature and the beautiful road itself.

Stage 4: Springfield (Missouri) - Oklahoma City (Oklahoma)

This stretch of the route is exciting because in just a few kilometers, you cross three different states: Missouri, a tiny piece of Kansas, and Oklahoma. It's fascinating to see how proud Kansas is to host that small section of Route 66.

Stage 5: Oklahoma City (Oklahoma) - Amarillo (Texas)

In this stage, we found one of our favorite cities on the route, Oklahoma City: clean, not too big, charming. We also found a hotel with mice. We ended this stage in Amarillo, dining at the Big Texan, a restaurant famous for its 72oz steak challenge. The challenge is to eat over 2 kilograms of meat with its corresponding side dish within an hour, and we were lucky to see a brave soul attempt it. In the post for this stage, we'll tell you if they succeeded.

Stage 6: Amarillo (Texas) - Santa Fe (New Mexico)

Going from Texas to New Mexico is impactful. It's like literally entering Mexico. It caught our attention a lot. Additionally, in this stage, we reached the Mid Point of Route 66 in Adrian. A super typical place on Route 66 that you must visit.

Stage 7: Santa Fe (New Mexico) - Gallup (New Mexico)

Perhaps this is one of the stages with fewer things to see on the road, but not less interesting. The road or the abandoned towns, showing how bustling they were when Route 66 was in its heyday, are well worth it.

Stage 8: Gallup (New Mexico) - Flagstaff (Arizona)

We did this stage with special excitement because we were reaching the gates of the Grand Canyon. Also, in Flagstaff, we found a place where we had a delicious sushi dinner. On the way, we found the marvelous Petrified Forest, a natural park where trees have turned into stone. Truly spectacular.

Stage 9: Flagstaff (Arizona) - Grand Canyon - Monument Valley - Big Water (Utah)

Once deviating from Route 66 for the second time, we fully immersed ourselves in the great natural parks, one of the travel areas we were most eager to explore. This stage (we woke up at 4 in the morning) and the next will be the toughest because they are the longest and most intense, but still incredible.

Stage 10: Big Water (Utah) - Horseshoe Bend - Antelope Canyon - Bryce Canyon - Zion Park - Las Vegas (Nevada)

Without a doubt, this stage is the most intense of all on the route, not only because it is the longest. We went from the immense grandeur of nature at Horseshoe Bend to the extreme luxury of Las Vegas in just a few hours. Essential. A piece of us stayed in those places.

Stage 11: Las Vegas (Nevada) - Santa Monica (California)

And here comes the end of the route... Santa Monica! With regret, we say goodbye to all the kilometers we have traveled to get here (although we continue the journey to San Francisco). In this stage, you will breathe in California to the fullest.

Day 1 Hometown - Chicago

Embark on a journey from your hometown to the vibrant city of Chicago. After arrival, complete necessary procedures, secure your rental car, and immerse yourself in the city's diverse offerings. Explore iconic landmarks and savor Chicago's renowned cuisine before finding accommodation for a restful night.

Day 2 Chicago - Wilmington - Braidwood - Pontiac - Springfield IL

Embark on the legendary Route 66, exploring nostalgic gems like Lou Mitchell's, Gemini Giant, and Old Log Cabin. Visit Springfield, Illinois, discovering President Abraham Lincoln's legacy at the Presidential Library. Enjoy local specialties before retiring for the night.

Day 3 Springfield IL - Cahokia Mounds - Collinsville - Saint Louis MO

Resume your road trip, exploring Springfield's historical landmarks and then traversing south on Route 66. Pause at Cahokia Mounds, an intriguing national park showcasing a prehistoric Indian city. Continue towards Saint Louis, crossing the Chain of Rocks Bridge. As the city beckons, delve into 'The Hill,' an Italian neighborhood, and savor its culinary delights. The day culminates with an overnight stay in Saint Louis.

Day 4 Saint Louis MO - Hannibal - Galesburg

Discover Saint Louis attractions, including the iconic Gateway Arch. Head north to Hannibal, Mark Twain's childhood home. Galesburg, home to poet Carl Sandburg, offers a charming retreat. Explore the historic Seminary Street district, steeped in Midwestern history. Conclude the day with a peaceful night's rest in Galesburg.

Day 5 Galesburg - Moline - Galena - Madison

Embark on a scenic drive from Galesburg to Moline, Galena, and finally Madison. Galena's historic downtown beckons exploration, and Madison's modern charm captivates with its Capitol building and contemporary art museum. Delight in a varied gastronomic experience along State Street. Find comfort in accommodation within Madison.

Day 6 Madison - Milwaukee

Journey west to Milwaukee, a city with a rich industrial past. Uncover the city's history at Miller Brewery and Harley-Davidson facilities. Enjoy a stroll in Lakefront Park and explore districts like Third Ward. Optional visits to Milwaukee Art Museum or Discovery World add cultural flair. Conclude the day with a taste of local fish specialties and a night's stay in Milwaukee.

Day 7 Milwaukee - Chicago

Explore Milwaukee further, then journey back to Chicago, tracing the shores of Lake Michigan. Immerse yourself in Chicago's cultural offerings, from museums to parks. Savor the city's renowned deep-dish pizza and embrace its vibrant nightlife. Choose from a myriad of experiences, including a visit to Al Capone's classic "Green Mill" bar. Retire for the night in the bustling city of Chicago.

Day 8 Chicago - Hometown

Presentation at the airport well in advance to return the rental car and board the return flight to the hometown. Overnight on board.
